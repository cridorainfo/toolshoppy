Dubai Gold Rate AED & INR Guide

Updated 15 July 2026 · 7 min read · Gold Rates

Dubai is one of the world's largest gold trading hubs. For the millions of Indians living in the UAE — especially Keralites in Dubai, Sharjah, and Abu Dhabi — tracking the daily gold rate in dirhams (AED) and converting it to rupees (INR) is essential for wedding shopping, festival purchases, and deciding whether to buy gold in the Gulf or back home in Kerala.

This guide explains how Dubai gold rates work, how to compare AED and INR prices, and what NRIs should know before buying.

How Dubai gold rates are quoted

UAE jewellers display gold per gram in AED, typically for:

  • 24K (999) — pure gold, bars and coins
  • 22K (916) — standard jewellery purity in Dubai and India alike
  • 21K (875) — popular in some Gulf traditional designs

Dubai rates track international spot gold closely because the UAE charges zero VAT on investment gold bars and coins (jewellery carries 5% VAT as of 2026 — verify at purchase). The Dubai Gold & Jewellery Group publishes a daily benchmark that most Deira and Gold Souk shops reference.

Converting AED gold price to INR

To compare Dubai gold with Kerala or Mumbai rates, you need two conversions:

  1. Gold price in AED — from the Dubai rate page (e.g., AED 320/gram for 22K)
  2. AED to INR exchange rate — use ToolShoppy's Currency Converter (e.g., 1 AED = ₹23.20)
  3. Multiply: 320 × 23.20 = ₹7,424 per gram equivalent

Compare that ₹7,424 with today's Kerala 22K rate. The cheaper market depends on the day's exchange rate, local making charges, and import duties — not just the raw per-gram gold price.

Dubai vs Kerala — what NRIs consider

  • Making charges: Dubai competitive for plain gold; Kerala better for intricate temple jewellery craftsmanship
  • VAT: 5% on UAE jewellery — factor into total cost
  • Customs duty: Bringing gold to India beyond duty-free allowance (currently 50g for male passengers, 100g for female — verify latest customs rules) attracts import duty
  • Exchange rate timing: When INR is weak, Dubai gold looks expensive in rupee terms even if AED price is stable
  • Purpose: Investment bars vs wedding jewellery — different decision frameworks

Best places to check rates in Dubai

Deira Gold Souk remains the iconic market, but mall chains offer fixed transparent pricing with hallmarked BIS-equivalent UAE standards. Always ask for the day's gold rate per gram before discussing designs — the rate board should be visible in the shop.

Festival and wedding season patterns

Gold demand spikes during Onam, Diwali, Akshaya Tritiya, and UAE National Day sales. NRIs often coordinate purchases during Dubai Shopping Festival (DSF) for promotional making-charge discounts. Track rates for two weeks before large buys — a ₹200/gram swing on a 50-gram chain is ₹10,000.

Sending gold home vs remittance

Some NRIs buy Dubai gold bars for family in Kerala; others send INR via remittance and let family buy locally. Compare all-in costs: Dubai purchase price + VAT + travel customs duty vs Kerala rate + local making charges. The math changes monthly with forex movement.

Quick daily routine for NRIs

  1. Check Dubai gold rate (AED per gram)
  2. Convert to INR using the currency tool
  3. Compare with Kerala rate
  4. Decide whether to buy now or wait for a dip

Gold is a long-term purchase — a morning rate check takes 30 seconds and can save thousands of dirhams on a wedding season shopping trip.
